Soil Types & Testing

  • Yellow Soil: Contains iron oxides,good for wheat and maize.
  • Black Soil: Rich in nutrients, suitable for cereals.
  • Laterite Soil: Rich in iron , great for most crops.
  • Peaty Soil: High organic matter, perfect for growing vegetables.

Additional Soil Information

Soil pH: Ideal range for crops is 6.0-7.5.

Soil Moisture: Check moisture levels regularly for optimal crop growth.

Organic Matter: Helps retain soil moisture and nutrients.

Soil Texture: Affects water retention and aeration.

Soil Erosion: Prevent through cover crops and no-till farming.

Soil Fertility: Enhance with organic fertilizers and compost.

Compaction: Aerate compacted soil to improve root growth.

Soil Drainage: Poor drainage can lead to root rot.

Soil Testing: Test soil every few years to monitor nutrient levels.

Soil Structure: Healthy structure promotes water infiltration.

Soil Conservation: Practices to maintain soil health and prevent degradation.

Crop Rotation: Helps prevent nutrient depletion and pest buildup.
